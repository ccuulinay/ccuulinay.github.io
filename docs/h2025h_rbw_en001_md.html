# HSBC 2025 Annual Report Analysis ## From the Perspective of Head of Retail Banking & Wealth Management, Hang Seng Bank **Report Date:** March 2, 2026 **Subject:** Competitive Analysis & Strategic Implications for Hang Seng Bank --- ## Executive Summary HSBC's 2025 annual results, announced on February 25, 2026, reveal a banking giant successfully navigating market volatility while doubling down on its strategic priorities. With reported profit before tax of **$29.9 billion** (underlying $36.6 billion excluding notable items), HSBC demonstrated particular strength in **wealth management** and **Hong Kong operations** – areas of direct relevance to Hang Seng Bank's competitive positioning. The landmark completion of the **Hang Seng Bank privatization** (January 26, 2026) for $13.7 billion fundamentally reshapes our relationship, transforming us from a listed associate to a wholly-owned subsidiary. This creates both opportunities for deeper integration and imperatives for strategic differentiation. --- ## 1. Product-by-Product Analysis ### 2.1 Deposits **HSBC Performance:** - Total customer deposits: **$1,787 billion** (+4% YoY, +$78bn) - Loan-to-deposit ratio: **55%** (highly liquid position) - Hong Kong segment deposits: **$540+ billion** (+$37bn growth) - Hong Kong time deposits: 36% of total deposits (Q4 2025) **Competitive Implications for Hang Seng:** - HSBC's 25% market share in Hong Kong deposits represents formidable scale - Strong deposit growth ($37bn in HK alone) suggests continued customer trust in the HSBC brand - The 36% time deposit ratio indicates rate-sensitive customers – opportunity for Hang Seng to differentiate with flexible products **Customer Behavior Trend:** Deposit growth driven by "capital and wealth inflows" suggests HSBC is benefiting from regional wealth migration to Hong Kong. --- ### 2.2 Personal Loans & Credit Cards **HSBC Performance:** - Total personal loan book: **$525 billion** - **90% secured lending** (strong credit quality focus) - Portfolio breakdown: - Mortgages: 81.4% (avg LTV: 19.1% HK, 16.7% UK) - Credit cards: 4.4% of personal lending - Other secured: 5.6% - Other unsecured: 8.6% - Credit quality: 76% of loans classified as 'Strong' or 'Good' **Competitive Implications for Hang Seng:** - HSBC's conservative 90% secured lending approach leaves room in the unsecured lending market - Low credit card penetration (4.4%) suggests either untapped potential or strategic de-emphasis - Very low LTV ratios (16-19%) indicate room for more aggressive secured lending products **Customer Behavior Trend:** HSBC's de-risking strategy (90% secured) may push credit-worthy customers seeking unsecured products to competitors. --- ### 2.3 Investment Products & Wealth Management **HSBC Performance:** - Wealth fee income: **$9.4 billion** (+24% YoY) - Invested assets: **$1,535 billion** (+19% YoY) - Asia: $773 billion (+20%) - Wealth balances: **$1,563 billion** (+15% YoY) - Asia: $1,055 billion (+16%) - Net new money (Q4 2025): **$26 billion** (Asia: $19bn) - Premier customers: **1 million** (+11% YoY) - New affluent clients added: **275,000** **Fee Income Growth by Category (Q4 2025 vs Q4 2024):** | Category | Growth | |----------|--------| | Investment Distribution | +14% | | Asset Management | +20% | | Private Banking | +14% | | Insurance | +49% | **Hong Kong Wealth Specifically:** - Wealth fees surged **40%** year-over-year - Hong Kong segment delivered **35.5% RoTE** (highest in group) - HSBC's wealth fee income per retail customer is now **35% higher** than Hang Seng (widened from 10% in 2024) **Competitive Implications for Hang Seng:** - The 35% fee income gap is widening – urgent need to enhance wealth product offerings - HSBC's 64 Wealth Centres across Asia (27 new in 2025) creates physical network advantage - Strong insurance growth (+49%) highlights HSBC's bancassurance strength - The $2.1 trillion total wealth balances (with $1tn+ in Asia) demonstrates HSBC's scale that Hang Seng must compete against **Customer Behavior Trend:** Strong demand for diversified investment products amid geopolitical uncertainties; customers seeking cross-border wealth solutions. --- ### 2.4 Insurance **HSBC Performance:** - Insurance manufacturing CSM balance: **$14.6 billion** (Q4 2025, up from $12.1bn) - Insurance fee income growth: **+49%** (Q4 2025 vs Q4 2024) - Integrated into broader wealth strategy showing "strong, broad-based growth" **Competitive Implications for Hang Seng:** - HSBC's insurance manufacturing capability (CSM: $14.6bn) is a significant competitive moat - Hang Seng may need to strengthen bancassurance partnerships or develop manufacturing capabilities - 49% growth rate suggests underpenetrated market opportunity **Customer Behavior Trend:** Increasing demand for protection and savings products as part of holistic wealth planning. --- ## 3. Strategic Developments with Direct Relevance to Hang Seng ### 3.1 Hang Seng Bank Privatization (Completed January 26, 2026) **Deal Terms:** - Acquisition price: **$13.7 billion** - Removes $3.8 billion in minority capital inefficiency - Expected synergies: **$0.8 billion annually** ($0.3bn cost, $0.5bn revenue) - Strategic rationale: "Capture growth in the high-returning Hong Kong market" **Implications for Hang Seng:** - **Opportunity:** Access to HSBC's global network, product shelf, and technology platforms - **Opportunity:** Cross-referral potential between HSBC One and Hang Seng customer bases - **Risk:** Potential brand dilution or product overlap - **Imperative:** Need to define clear value proposition distinct from HSBC Premier ### 3.2 Organizational Simplification **HSBC Actions:** - Targeting **8% reduction in payroll costs** - Net **15% reduction in Managing Director positions** - 11 market exits completed in 2025 - Cost savings target: $1.5 billion **Implications for Hang Seng:** - Potential for shared services and operational synergies - Risk of talent displacement affecting morale - Opportunity to absorb high-quality talent from HSBC's restructuring --- ## 4.
