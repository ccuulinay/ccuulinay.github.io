# HSBC 2025 Annual Report Analysis: Retail Banking and Wealth Management Perspective for Hang Seng Bank ## Executive Summary This report analyzes HSBC's 2025 Annual Report and the Hong Kong Budget 2026 to provide strategic insights and recommendations for Hang Seng Bank's retail banking and wealth management operations. HSBC delivered strong financial performance in 2025 with reported profit before tax of $29.9 billion, while navigating complex global economic conditions. The HK Budget 2026 introduces significant initiatives that present both opportunities and challenges for retail banking and wealth management in Hong Kong. **Key Findings:** - HSBC's Hong Kong operations contributed $13.0 billion in profit before tax (up 9.6% YoY) - Wealth balances reached $2.14 trillion globally, with Hong Kong contributing $1.18 trillion - Net new invested assets of $80 billion generated in 2025 - HK Budget 2026 emphasizes RMB internationalization, digital assets, green finance, and wealth management centre development --- ## 1. Industry Overview and Market Context ### 1.1 Global Economic Environment According to HSBC's 2025 Annual Report, global growth in 2025 was stronger than expected, driven by: - AI capital expenditure and trade growth momentum - Resilient US consumer spending - Robust consumption and rising exports in ASEAN markets - Economic diversification in the Middle East with significant infrastructure investments However, challenges persist including: - Geopolitical uncertainty and trade policy changes - Continued conflicts affecting global stability - Divergent regulatory standards across jurisdictions - Commercial real estate market pressures in Hong Kong and mainland China ### 1.2 Hong Kong Banking Landscape HSBC maintains a leading market position in Hong Kong, comprising: - Retail Banking and Wealth operations of HSBC Hong Kong - Hang Seng Bank (now a wholly-owned subsidiary of HSBC Asia Pacific) - Commercial Banking services **Hong Kong Performance Highlights (2025):** - Profit before tax: $9.9 billion (business operations) / $13.0 billion (total contribution) - IWPB (International Wealth and Personal Banking) profit: $1.95 billion - CIB HK profit: $1.77 billion - Strong deposit momentum with growth in customer accounts --- ## 2. Product and Service Analysis ### 2.1 Credit Cards #### HSBC Performance and Trends - Credit card lending is a key component of personal lending portfolio - Forborne credit card loans: $191 million (stage 1 and 2 combined) - Credit quality remains stable with strong risk management - Digital transformation driving customer acquisition and engagement #### Strategic Implications for Hang Seng Bank **Opportunities:** 1. **Digital-First Card Propositions**: Leverage HK Budget's fintech initiatives to develop innovative card products with embedded digital features 2. **Cross-Border Payment Solutions**: Align with HK Budget's RMB internationalization push to offer enhanced RMB credit card services 3. **Lifestyle Ecosystem Integration**: Build partnerships within the Budget's "tourism + sports + culture" framework for card rewards **Challenges:** 1. Intense competition from virtual banks and fintech competitors 2. Interest rate margin compression affecting card profitability 3. Regulatory requirements on responsible lending **Recommendations:** - Launch co-branded cards targeting specific customer segments (professionals, Mainland visitors, green consumers) - Implement AI-powered credit decisioning to enhance approval speed and risk management - Develop tokenized payment solutions leveraging HKMA's Project Ensemble ### 2.2 Personal Loans #### HSBC Performance and Trends - Personal lending (secured and unsecured) showing stable performance - Forborne personal loans: $592 million total - Secured personal lending: $146 million - Unsecured personal lending: $446 million - Average LTV on new mortgage lending in Hong Kong: 70% - Strong focus on collateral quality and risk management #### Strategic Implications for Hang Seng Bank **Opportunities:** 1. **Tax Relief-Linked Lending**: Align with Budget's tax reduction measures ($3,000 salaries tax ceiling) to promote tax-season loan products 2. **Green Loan Products**: Leverage Budget's green finance initiatives to develop sustainable personal loans 3. **Digital Asset-Backed Lending**: Explore opportunities from the Budget's digital asset licensing framework **Challenges:** 1. Elevated household debt levels in Hong Kong 2. Commercial real estate market volatility affecting secured lending 3. Regulatory scrutiny on lending practices **Recommendations:** - Develop purpose-specific loan products (education, healthcare, green renovation) tied to Budget initiatives - Implement dynamic pricing models based on customer relationship depth - Enhance digital application processes with instant approval capabilities ### 2.3 Deposits #### HSBC Performance and Trends - Total deposits: $1.88 trillion globally - Strong deposit growth in Asia, including mainland China and Hong Kong - Net interest income: $34.8 billion (up $2.1 billion YoY) - Wealth deposits (Premier and Private Bank): $608 billion globally - Hong Kong wealth deposits: $407 billion - Deposit balance growth supported by market volatility inflows #### Strategic Implications for Hang Seng Bank **Opportunities:** 1. **RMB Deposit Products**: Leverage Budget's doubling of RMB Business Facility to RMB 200 billion 2. **Tokenized Deposit Solutions**: Participate in HKMA's Project Ensemble for deposit innovation 3. **Cross-Border Deposit Services**: Serve increasing Mainland-Hong Kong financial flows **Challenges:** 1. Interest rate compression affecting deposit margins 2. Competition from high-yield savings products in the market 3. Customer migration to higher-yielding investment products **Recommendations:** - Develop tiered deposit products linked to wealth management relationships - Introduce RMB-denominated deposit products with competitive rates - Create digital-only deposit accounts with enhanced interest rates ### 2.4 Investment Services #### HSBC Performance and Trends - Total reported wealth balances: $2.14 trillion - Invested assets: $1.54 trillion globally - Hong Kong invested assets: $773 billion - Net new invested assets: $80 billion (2025) - Hong Kong net new invested assets: $39 billion - Private Bank client balances: $566 billion - Asset Management third-party distribution: $580 billion #### Strategic Implications for Hang Seng Bank **Opportunities:** 1. **Family Office Services**: Capitalize on Budget measures for 3,300+ single-family offices in Hong Kong 2. **Digital Asset Investment**: Prepare for licensed digital asset trading and custody services 3. **Green and Sustainable Investments**: Align with Budget's green finance initiatives 4. **REIT Investment Opportunities**: Benefit from proposed REIT mutual access inclusion **Challenges:** 1. Market volatility affecting investment confidence 2. Regulatory complexity for cross-border investment products 3. Fee compression in asset management **Recommendations:** - Develop dedicated family office and ultra-high-net-worth solutions - Launch ESG-themed investment portfolios aligned with Hong Kong Sustainability Disclosure Standards - Create digital wealth management platforms with robo-advisory capabilities - Expand RMB-denominated investment product offerings ### 2.5 Insurance #### HSBC Performance and Trends - Insurance business integrated within wealth management offering - Disposal of French life insurance business and German private banking life insurance - Focus on core markets including Hong Kong - Bancassurance model driving distribution efficiency #### Strategic Implications for Hang Seng Bank **Opportunities:** 1. **Health and Wellness Products**: Align with Budget's healthcare initiatives 2. **Retirement Planning Solutions**: Address aging population needs with MPF and annuity products 3. **Cyber Insurance**: Support digital transformation with protection products **Challenges:** 1. Regulatory changes affecting product design and distribution 2. Low interest rate environment impacting product profitability 3. Changing customer risk preferences **Recommendations:** - Develop integrated wealth-protection solutions combining investment and insurance - Launch parametric insurance products for digital asset risks - Create longevity planning solutions for aging demographics --- ## 3. HK Budget 2026 Key Points Relevant to Retail Banking and Wealth Management ### 3.1 Financial Services Development #### RMB Internationalization (Paragraphs 91-92) - **Doubling RMB Business Facility to RMB 200 billion** to support trade and cross-boundary business - More convenient foreign exchange quotations between RMB and regional currencies - Regular issuance of RMB bonds to enrich offshore RMB market - Exploring offshore RMB yield curve formation - Attracting high-quality issuers to increase RMB bond issuance **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Expand RMB product offerings across deposits, loans, and investments - Develop RMB hedging and forex solutions for corporate and retail clients - Participate in RMB bond issuance and distribution #### Asset and Wealth Management Centre Development (Paragraph 98) - **Over 3,300 single-family offices** in Hong Kong - Tax regime enhancements including: - Expanding "fund" scope to cover specific funds-of-one - Classifying digital assets, precious metals, commodities as qualifying investments - Implementation from year of assessment 2025/26 **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Develop dedicated family office service teams - Create digital asset investment solutions - Enhance precious metals and commodities trading capabilities #### REIT Market Development (Paragraph 99) - Seeking inclusion of REITs under mutual-market access - Amendment bill to enable REIT privatisation/restructuring - Stamp duty waiver for non-residential property transfers into REITs **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Develop REIT-focused investment products - Provide REIT financing and advisory services - Create property-to-REIT structuring solutions ### 3.2 Fintech and Digital Assets (Paragraphs 104-110) #### Digital Asset Licensing Regime - Bill to establish licensing for digital asset dealing and custodian service providers - Fiat-referenced stablecoin licensing regime with first licenses to be issued - SFC to enhance digital asset market liquidity and facilitate more products for professional investors - SFC accelerator to expedite market innovation **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Apply for digital asset dealing and custody licenses - Develop stablecoin payment and settlement solutions - Create digital asset investment products for qualified investors #### Project Ensemble (Paragraph 109) - HKMA launched pilot for tokenised deposits and digital assets - 24/7 settlement capability development - Local standards development for interoperability **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Participate in Project Ensemble pilot programs - Develop tokenised deposit products - Explore digital asset collateralized lending ### 3.3 Support Measures for Citizens and Businesses (Paragraphs 279-280) #### Tax Relief Measures - Salaries tax reduction: 100% up to $3,000 ceiling (2025/26) - Profits tax reduction: 100% up to $3,000 ceiling - Basic allowance increase: $132,000 to $145,000 (from 2026/27) - Child allowance increase: $130,000 to $140,000 - Dependent parent allowance increase **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Develop tax-season promotional campaigns - Create tax-optimized investment and savings products - Offer tax payment financing solutions #### Social Security Support - One-month allowance for CSSA, OAA, OALA, DA recipients - Working Family Allowance enhancements **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Develop financial products targeting lower-income segments - Create micro-lending and savings programs - Provide financial literacy education ### 3.4 Green Finance (Paragraphs 202-204) - Continued sustainable bond issuance - Hong Kong Taxonomy for Sustainable Finance refinement - Green transition planning guidance for banks - Exploration of Green Technology Projects Accelerator **Implications for Hang Seng Bank:** - Expand green loan and mortgage offerings - Develop sustainability-linked deposit products - Create green investment funds --- ## 4.
